About

M&G Biosystems is a Biomedical research group, formed by Maya Laughton and Gonzalo Miranda - two Senior undergraduates at Tufts Univesity School of Engineering. M&G Biosystems currently partners with the Maguire Lab at Tufts University School of Medicine to conduct in vitro analysis of the correlation between gamma oscillations in the amygdala with learned fear conditioning, as well as to create an in silico model of these observed neural rhythms.

Guidance

Maya Laughton and Gonzalo Miranda formed M&G Biosystems when designing their Senior Capstone, as undergraduate Biomedical Engineers at Tufts University. Combining their love of neuroscience, biomedical engineering, and computer science, Maya and Gonzalo work on the frontier of neural studies. Under the guidance of Dr. Jamie Maguire at Tufts University Medical School, Dr. Christoph Borgers at Tufts University, and Dr. Brian Timko at Tufts University School of Engineering, we are designing an in silico model evaluating gamma oscillations in the amygdala, with respect to learned fear conditioning.
